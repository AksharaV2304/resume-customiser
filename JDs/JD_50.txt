Job Summary:
We are seeking a highly skilled and motivated Retail Credit Risk Analytics Model Developer to join our team. The successful candidate will play a critical role in designing, developing, and implementing advanced predictive models that support risk management decisions for our retail credit portfolio.

Key Responsibilities:
1. Develop, validate, and implement new retail credit risk models using Python and other relevant statistical and machine learning tools.
2. Collaborate with cross-functional teams to understand business needs and translate them into model requirements.
3. Perform data analysis, model calibration, and scenario testing to ensure the accuracy and reliability of credit risk models.
4. Contribute to the ongoing maintenance and enhancement of existing models, ensuring compliance with regulatory requirements.
5. Provide expertise in credit risk management, including credit scoring, portfolio management, and stress testing.
6. Participate in model validation activities and provide documentation for regulatory submissions.
7. Stay abreast of industry trends and advancements in credit risk modeling and machine learning techniques.
8. Collaborate with IT to ensure seamless integration of models into the company's technology infrastructure.

Qualifications:
1. Master's degree or higher in Statistics, Mathematics, Computer Science, Economics, Finance, or a related field.
2. Proven experience in credit risk modeling, preferably within a retail banking environment.
3. Expertise in Python and familiarity with other programming languages (e.g., R, SQL).
4. Strong knowledge of statistical and machine learning techniques, including regression analysis, decision trees, neural networks, and ensemble methods.
5. Familiarity with credit risk modeling frameworks such as PD/LGD/EAD, Basel III, and CCAR.
6. Excellent analytical, problem-solving, and communication skills.
7. Ability to work independently and collaboratively in a fast-paced environment.
8. Strong understanding of regulatory requirements related to credit risk modeling (e.g., Dodd-Frank Act, Basel III).
